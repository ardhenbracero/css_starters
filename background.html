Backgrounds

CSS Background allow you to style the background for elements or body with colors and images.

background-attachment

Defines how the background will response when scrolling the web page.

background-attachment: scroll;

The background image will scroll along with the page. Additionally, it will adjust its size and location to fit the element to which it is applied.

. . .

Sample text!

background-attachment: fixed;

The background picture will not move with the page and will stay in the viewport's position. Additionally, it will adjust its size and location to fit the viewport. Therefore, it's likely that the background image will only be partially visible.

. . .

Sample text!

background-color

Defines the color of the element's background.

background-color: lightblue;

Also in background-color you call also use over 140+ color names.

. . .

Sample text!

background-color: #5e2287;

Also in background-color you call also use over hexadecimal code.

. . .

Sample text!

background-color: rgb(241, 243, 94);

Also in background-color you call also use over rgb

  • The first values is R meaning is red.
  • The second values is G meaning is green.
  • The third values is B meaning is blue.
Each of them can have a value up to 0 - 255

background-image

Defines an image as the background of an element.

background-image: url(images/css-logo.png);

Utilizes the image specified by the url path. This route may be either be relative.

. . .

Sample text!

background-image: linear-gradient(pink, skyblue);

You can define a linear gradient as the background image. There must be a minimum of two defined colors.

The bottom one will begin first, followed by the top one.

The gradient is vertical, strating at the top and terminating at the bottom of the element when the default angle is to bottom (or 180deg).

. . .

Sample text!

background-image: linear-gradient(35 deg, pink, skyblue);

You can specify an angle,either in degree or with keywords.

When using degress, you indicate the gradient's direction or its terminus. Therefore, 0deg refers to the element's top, or like 12:00 on a clock.

In this example, 35deg means 2:30 or the top right coner.

. . .

Sample text!

background-image: radial-gradient(pink, skyblue);

You can also define a radial gradient as a background image.

You also need to define two colors the first color will be at the center, while the second color will be at the edges.

background-position

Defines the position of the background image.

background-position: bottom left;

You can use a combination of position keywords. center, top, bottom, left and right.

. . .

Sample text!

background-position: center center;

The background image shall be positioned at the center of the element.

background-repeat

Sets the background position as the starting point for the background image to repeat across the backdrop of the element.

background-repeat: repeat;

The background image will repeat itself.

. . .

Sample text!

background-repeat: repeat-x;

The background image will repeat vertically.

. . .

Sample text!

background-repeat: repeat-y;

The background image will repeat horizontally .

. . .

Sample text!

background-repeat: no-repeat;

The background image will appear once..

. . .

Sample text!

background-size

Sets the background size of an image.

background-size: auto auto;

The background will retain its original size.

. . .

Sample text!

background-size: 150px 100px;

You also can specify a size using pixels

  • first value is horizontally
  • second value is vertically

. . .

Sample text!

background-size: cover;

The backdrop image will be resized by the keyword cover to ensure that the element is completely covered.
